# Music-Store-Project002

# Project Overview
This project analyzes sales data from a music store to understand customer behavior, product performance, and overall business growth. The analysis helps identify top-selling products, high-value customers, and revenue-generating genres.
The insights can be used to improve inventory management and marketing strategies.
# Business Objectives
Analyze overall sales performance
Identify top-selling artists and genres
Understand customer purchase behavior
Determine high-value customers
Support business decisions for product strategy
# Dataset Description
The dataset includes:
Customer details
Invoice and purchase records
Track and genre information
Artist and album data
Sales revenue
# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Total Sales Revenue
Number of Customers
Total Transactions
Revenue by Genre
Revenue by Artist
Top Customers by Spend
# Methodology
Imported and explored relational database tables
Wrote SQL queries to answer business-driven questions
Aggregated sales and customer data
Identified trends and patterns in purchasing behavior
Converted query results into business insights
# Key Insights
Certain music genres contributed the majority of revenue
A small group of customers generated a large portion of total sales
Popular artists significantly influenced store performance
Repeat customers showed higher lifetime value
Genre preference varied across customer segments
# Business Recommendations
Promote top-performing genres and artists
Offer loyalty benefits for high-value customers
Introduce bundled offers for slow-moving inventory
Personalize marketing based on genre preference
Optimize inventory based on demand patterns
# Conclusion
This project shows how SQL-based analysis can help retail businesses understand their customers and improve sales strategies. The findings support data-driven decisions for marketing, inventory, and customer relationship management.
